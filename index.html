My Favorite Food

Sinigang

Ingredients

  • Main Ingredient
    • Pork/Beef/Shirmp/Fish/Chicken
    • Tamarind
  • Vegetables
    • Okra
    • Taro (Gabi) (to thicken the broth)
    • Radish
    • Water Spinach (Kangkong)
    • String Beans (Sitaw)
    • Eggplant (Talong)
  • Aromatics
    • Onion
    • Tomatoes
    • Garlic
  • Seasonings
    • Fish Sauce (Patis)
    • Salt
    • Long Green Chili Peppers (Siling haba) (For a bit of spice)

The Process of Making Sinigang

  1. Sauté Aromatics:

    • In a large pot, Sauté garlic and onions until fragrants.
    • Add tomatoes and cook until they soften,,

  2. Add Protein:

    • Add your choice of protein (Pork, Beef, Shrimp, Fish, or Chicken) and cook until it changes color.
    • For pork, some recipes recommend searing the pork belly before adding the water to develop a deeper flavor.

  3. Pour in Water:

    • Fill the pot with enough water to cover the Ingredients.
    • Bring to a boil and then reduce to a simmer.

  4. Introduce the Souring Agent:

    • Add tamarind pulp or powder to create the signature sour taste.
    • If using tamarind pulp, squeeze it into the pot and let it dissolve. If using powder, add to taste.
    • Alternatively, use a sinigang mix for convenience.

  5. Add Vegetables:

    • Toss in vegetables like radish, taro, eggplant, okra, string beans, and water spinach (kangkong),,.
    • Add vegetables that require longer cooking times (like taro and radish) earlier, and leafy greens (like kangkong or spinach) towards the end.

  6. Simmer:

    • Let the soup simmer until the meat and vegetables are tender,.
    • This typically takes about 30-45 minutes for the meat to become tender.

  7. Season to Perfection:

    • Season with Fish Sauce (Patis) and salt according to your preference,,.
    • Add chilli peppers if you want a bit of heat,.

  8. Serve Hot:

    • Ladle the fragrant, tangy soup into bowls and serve it piping hot with freshly cooked rice,,.
